Chapter 1: Intelligence

Summary:

Division 8 of The Mist Special Investigation Unit has always been a laidback workplace, with it being placed in a considerably remote place with little to no action for their line of work. Until a powerful demon appeared with intelligence and cunning when they least expect it.

Clacks of multiple keyboards echoed throughout the small office room. The shine of the sun partially blocked by the curtains, leaving rays still going through for the strategically placed potted plants. Clock ticking admist the shuffles of the stacks of papers that are on almost each of the staff’s table, with the printer ejecting more paper.

Grunts and frustrated sighs were heard a couple of times before it was broken by a small ding of the digital clock situated nearest to the manager’s— or Heinrich’s table.

Heinrich looked over to the clock and knocked three times on his table, bringing everyone’s attention to him.

“30 minutes until daily patrol. Three people should be enough.”

Yura stood up to looked over to the blonde and faced him. Her chair squeaking against the floor. “We usually send out only two for daily patrols, is something the matter?”

Heinrich gave a sigh and massaged his temples. As he did so he answered, “There has been reports from other divisions of more demons appearing in the mist, instead of just one.” He paused and stopped rubbing his temples, before continuing, “Although rare and the levels have been all low in those cases, we still need to be cautious.”

The room went silent for a period until another squeak of chair was heard. Sybill took her dagger from the weapon rack. Yura joined her and grabbed her own spear.

“Today was Yura’s and my turn for patrol. Who’s the third person?”

Heinrich stayed silent and glanced over to the man that has been bothering Alka. He took notice and cocked his head torwards Heinrich.

“Yes you, Cassian. You’re the only close ranged staff that has not done patrol this week.”

Cassian shrugged and stood up from his seat, joining the two girls who has been on standby. He grabbed his double-headed spear.
